getting into it a little deeper, the gameplay is a blend of star citizen, gta, and your favorite turn-based strategy games. As a member of one of 3 different factions, you will have the option of doing any specific or combination of things including but not limited to:

    ship building
    resource gathering
    piracy
    trading
    security
    transportation

the game is set in space that is split into 3 zones: low, medium, and high risk with varying rules and consequences to each.

Yes. unlike games like fifa or gta that use worthless in game currency that you spend real money on to buy digital goods that you cant even sell to other players for neither real nor digital currency, star atlas is built on 2 (span text to emphasize & also include a link to solana docs) solana program library tokens.

    polis
    atlas

These 2 tokens live on the solana blockchain and are composable in the network between all users (whether they play or not), and (a link to an article about) defi protocols like (a links to) tulip francium and step

ATLAS is used to acquire digital assets such as ships, crew, components, land, and equipment but it is also used for business operating expenses, such as personnel for mining equipment, fuel for ships, and repairs for damages.

Polis is the governance token for the yet to be implemented (a link to) the star atlas DAO, where voting weight will be distributed based on token ownership and decisions relating to in-game mechanics and also decisions like hiring 3rd party studios to improve/create new content will be made.
